## How to Choose a Standing Mat

- **Thickness:** 3/4" to 1" is the sweet spot — too thin and you lose cushioning; too thick and stability suffers
- **Material:** Polyurethane foam is durable and easy to clean; memory foam conforms more but can wear faster; natural rubber is eco-friendly and grippy
- **Flat vs contoured:** Flat mats are neutral and less distracting; contoured mats like the Topo actively promote movement
- **Size:** Match to your standing desk width — at minimum 20"×32", ideally wider if your desk allows
- **Floor type:** Check the non-slip backing is appropriate for your floor surface (some grips work better on hard floors, some on carpet)

## Do You Actually Need a Standing Mat?

If you stand for less than 30 minutes at a stretch, a mat is optional. If you stand for an hour or more at a time — which is where the real health benefit of a standing desk kicks in — a quality mat shifts from nice-to-have to essential. Without one, most people find standing uncomfortable enough that they stop doing it.
