## What to Look for in an Ergonomic Keyboard

Before buying, consider these factors:

- **Split vs. standard:** Split keyboards reduce wrist pronation; standard ergonomic keyboards still help via better key angles and wrist rests
- **Switch type:** Mechanical switches offer tactile feedback but are noisier; membrane and scissor switches are quieter
- **Wrist rest:** Some keyboards include built-in rests; others pair best with a separate wrist pad
- **Connectivity:** Wireless reduces cable clutter; wired eliminates battery concerns
- **Size:** Tenkeyless layouts bring your mouse closer and reduce shoulder strain
